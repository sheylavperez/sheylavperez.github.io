My story

Like many other immigrant stories, my parents came from Guatemala to New York for a better life, where I was born as the second oldest of six siblings. Growing up in a big, loving family, I learned the values of humility and gratitude. School wasn't always easy for me, especially in subjects like math and science, despite eventually majoring in math.

My Goal

I want to use my problem-solving and design skills to solve problems that most people neglect.

For example:

  1. Many apps lack inclusive language and features that cater to diverse cultural backgrounds.
  2. Facial recognition software has repeatedly shown bias against people of color, particularly darker-skinned individuals and women.
  3. Airbags and Seat Belts (Historically): Early automotive safety designs for seat belts and airbags were primarily tested on male crash test dummies and modeled around average male body sizes and shapes.

These are design flaws that are overlooked, and I want to be able to prevent them and represent customers so they feel seen and heard, enabling us to solve these issues.
