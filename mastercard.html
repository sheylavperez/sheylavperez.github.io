Project information

  • Category: Web design
  • Client: Mastercard
  • Scope: Product design, branding, and mobile app design
  • Project date: 01 March, 2020

Project Overview:

I am thrilled to spearhead the design for Mastercard's groundbreaking project - QR Payments, specifically tailored to empower vendors in the digital landscape. The primary goal is to simplify digital transactions, eliminate the need for vendor infrastructure, and seamlessly integrate businesses into the Mastercard ecosystem.


Project Specifications

Duration: 6 weeks

Tools

  • Figma
  • Miro

Deliverables

High-fidelity interactive prototypes for key tasks on website

UX/UI Design:

  • Competitive analysis
  • Personas
  • User journeys and task flows
  • High-fidelity mockups and prototypes
  • Visual Designer

Problems:

  • Traditional barriers for vendors in adapting to the digital payment landscape
  • Complexity in setting up infrastructure and integrating with existing systems
  • Limitations hindering vendors' full participation in the digital economy
  • Challenges faced by small and medium-sized businesses in accepting digital payments
  • Potential alienation of customers preferring digital payment methods due to vendor limitations

Proposed Solutions:

  • Simplifies digital payment acceptance for vendors
  • Eliminates the need for expensive infrastructure setup
  • Provides an intuitive mobile app interface tailored to vendors' needs
  • Streamlines the process of generating and managing QR codes for products
  • Integrates seamlessly with the Mastercard ecosystem
  • Enhances vendors' presence in the digital economy
  • Increases accessibility to digital payment methods for a diverse range of businesses
  • Improves customer experience by offering convenient payment options

By having a single website manage the business revenue, payments received, and QR code generation, users can easily track their financial transactions, streamline payment processes, and generate QR codes for their products or services.



Research

My research first centered around the competition space in order to understand how other apps are addressing similar issues. More than 100 user survey responses would help quantify what customers are paying for, and what they’re hoping to resolve. And lastly, one-on-one interviews would help me understand users’ journeys and specific issues the app would need to address.

Finding

  1. Resistance to Change: Businesses may face resistance from employees or stakeholders accustomed to traditional payment methods or subscription management systems.
  2. Regulatory Compliance: Businesses must ensure that their use of CashPass complies with relevant regulations and legal requirements, particularly regarding financial transactions, data privacy, and consumer protection laws.
  3. Security Concerns: Businesses may be concerned about the security of their financial data and transactions when using CashPass, especially if there are breaches or vulnerabilities in the system.

Getting Closer to User-Centered Design

Defining Key Differences in Motivations Through Personas

Finding

At first glance, it appeared that everyone shared a common interest: saving both money and time. However, detailed user research revealed diverse motivations among individuals. Creating personas proved invaluable in understanding these differences, serving as crucial reference points as features were developed. Throughout the research and design phases, I primarily concentrated on two personas. One focused heavily on scrutinizing usage reports and data to inform subscription decisions, while the other relied on the app's browse, search, and filter functions to explore viewing options.
