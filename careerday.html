The Wake-Up Call

"I chose my major because my parents said it's safe... Now I hate every class."

33% of students change majors, which can add $18,000 in tuition.

This heartfelt confession from a 19-year-old college sophomore during user interviews sparked our mission. With 33% of students changing majors (costing $10B annually in wasted tuition) and pandemic-induced career anxiety at record highs, we set out to reinvent career exploration for the TikTok generation.

The Problem

Many students struggle to find their career passions, leading to wasted time, resources, and increased dropout rates.

The project aimed to address the challenges faced by students:

  • Feelings of uncertainty
  • Limited exposure to diverse fields
  • Disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic

Deep Dive into the Maze

Through 47 student interviews and 300+ survey responses, we uncovered three pain points:

The "Google Paralysis" Effect

"Whenever I search 'good careers', I get overwhelmed by 10,000 options" - High School Junior

The LinkedIn Illusion

"All I see are people's success stories, never the daily grind" - College Freshman

The Guidance Gap

"Our school counselor has 800 students. She barely knows my name." - Community College Student

User's Pain Points

In summary, the user pain points revolve around the confusion and frustration that many students experience when navigating their career paths. These challenges arise from:

  • Lack of exposure to diverse fields.
  • One-third of students change majors during their college years.
  • Overwhelmed by complex career data.

What if Career Exploration Felt Like a Video Game

Core Insight: Gen Z needs to experience careers, not just read about them

Our Game Plan:

Career Simulation Quests

Day-in-the-life simulations (e.g., "Be a Cybersecurity Agent" with real hacking puzzles)

AI-Powered Roadmap Builder

Turn vague aspirations into actionable steps with skill mapping (e.g., "Your debate club experience = 60% to Lawyer role")

But why create an interactive platform?

Based on research, it shows that students are more interested in a career search from online platform.


  • The COVID-19 pandemic has disrupted traditional career exploration opportunities (job shadowing & internship) making virtual resources such as career exploration websites more important than ever before
  • According to a survey by Junior Achievement USA, 71% of high school students said that the pandemic has made them more interested in exploring career options online.

From Chaos to Clarity: The Iteration Journey

Version 1

Pivot Moment

Introduced gaming UI patterns after observing users spend 3X more time on Duolingo than our prototype.

Final Solution

Progress bars for career exploration completeness, achievement badges for skill development, avatar customization reflecting career paths.

Final Interface

Design of the career description interface, featuring progress bars to track career exploration. Users can visually assess their progress in exploring different career paths.

The AI-driven roadmap design, displaying personalized suggestions for users based on their career interests. Achievements and skill development are also tracked visually.

Gaming simulation interface, gamifying the career exploration process. Users are rewarded with achievement badges for completing milestones, enhancing engagement and learning.
