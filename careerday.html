Project Overview:

I am a UX designer currently working on two exciting projects, CareerDay and CUNY Clash. As a founder, I am dedicated to building this product from the ground up, utilizing my skills in product design, branding, and web design with Figma as the primary tool.

Problems:

  • Many students feel lost or uncertain about their career path
  • As evidenced by the fact that one-third of students change majors during their college years
  • Lack of exposure to diverse fields early on makes it tough for students to make informed decisions

Proposed Solutions:

  • "CareerDay" tackles this issue with an interactive platform offering insights into various careers, including day-to-day work, required skills, challenges, and an AI roadmap for goal achievement.
  • Visual aids, such as gaming simulations, provide the exposure needed to uncover one's true calling, transcending immediate constraints
  • Provides an intuitive mobile app interface tailored to vendors' needs

Imagine if you could step into a virtual world where you could explore different career options and get a hands-on experience of what it's really like to work in that field.

Research

    Initially, my research focused on the evolving landscape of career exploration, particularly in light of the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. This involved an extensive analysis of virtual resources such as career exploration websites, aiming to discern how they are addressing the shifting needs of users during these unprecedented times. Additionally, I conducted over 100 surveys to quantitatively gauge the preferences and priorities of users, shedding light on what aspects of career exploration are most valued and sought after.

Finding

  1. Adaptation to Remote Learning: The COVID-19 pandemic has disrupted traditional avenues for career exploration, such as job shadowing and internships, prompting a greater reliance on virtual resources like career exploration websites.
  2. Increased Interest in Online Resources: According to a survey conducted by Junior Achievement USA, 71% of high school students have expressed heightened interest in exploring career options online due to the pandemic's impact.

Getting Closer to User-Centered Design

Defining Key Differences in Motivations Through Personas

Finding

Initially, it seemed that all users had a unified goal of optimizing their career exploration experience for efficiency and effectiveness. However, in-depth user research uncovered a spectrum of motivations and preferences among individuals. Developing personas became indispensable in grasping these variations, offering essential guidance as we crafted features tailored to different user needs. During both the research and design stages, our attention was primarily drawn to two distinct personas. One persona delved deeply into analyzing usage reports and data to guide their career exploration and decision-making process, while the other relied heavily on the app's browsing, searching, and filtering functionalities to navigate through available career options.

Visualizing a User-Centric Experience

Engaging in rapid sketching enabled me to delve into prevalent design patterns found in competing apps, aiding in the identification of elements crucial for OneStream's user familiarity. Additionally, it facilitated the discovery of versatile screen types and intuitive swiping/touch gestures essential for seamless interaction
