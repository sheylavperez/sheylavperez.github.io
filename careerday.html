Navigating Careers: CareerDay's Innovative Gamified Platform


Project information

  • Category: Web design
  • Client: CareerDay
  • Scope: Product design, branding, and web design
  • Project Date: 01 March, 2020

Project Overview:

The objective of this project is to empower students by simplifying complex career information in a manner that resonates with Gen Z.

Role

The following roles designing this app:

  • Founder
  • Content Strategist
  • Frontend Developer
  • User Researcher
  • Interaction Designer

Project Specifications

Duration: 6 weeks

Tools

  • Figma
  • Miro
  • Google Slides
  • Balsamiq

Deliverables

High-fidelity interactive prototypes for key tasks on website

UX/UI Design:

  • Competitive analysis
  • Personas
  • High-fidelity mockups and prototypes


The Problem

The project aimed to address the challenges faced by students in navigating their career paths, including feelings of uncertainty, limited exposure to diverse fields, and disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. The project aimed to address the challenges faced by students in navigating their career paths, including feelings of uncertainty, limited exposure to diverse fields, and disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic



How can we solve this?


  • An interactive platform offering insights into various careers, including day-to-day work, required skills, challenges, and an AI roadmap for goal achievement.
  • Visual aids, such as gaming simulations, provide the exposure needed to uncover one's true calling, transcending immediate constraints
  • Provides an intuitive mobile app interface tailored to vendors' needs



Who would use this product?

Customers
  • Students
  • Parents
  • Educational institutions
  • Libraries
  • Career counselors
  • Service providers
  • Academic advisors
  • Guidance counselors

Example of a Student


Users Pain Points

  • Many students feel lost or uncertain about their career path
  • As evidenced by the fact that one-third of students change majors during their college years
  • Lack of exposure to diverse fields early on makes it tough for students to make informed decisions



But why create an interactive platform?

Based on research, it shows that students are more interested in a career search from online platform.




Thought Process

The wireframing process involves sketching various layouts for the platform's homepage. These wireframes outline the placement of navigation menus, search functionality, featured content sections, and calls-to-action. Through wireframing, I visualizes and iterates on the platform's design to create an intuitive and user-friendly experience for students exploring career options.

Final Outcome


Search Filter

  • Implement a search filter feature that allows users to refine their career exploration based on specific criteria such as industry, job title, location, or required skills.
  • Users can input their preferences and interests into the search filter to narrow down the list of available career options, making it easier to find relevant opportunities.

AI Roadmap:

  • Develop an AI-driven roadmap feature that provides users with personalized career guidance and goal-setting assistance
  • The AI algorithm analyzes user input, career preferences, and skills to generate a customized roadmap outlining recommended steps and milestones for achieving career objectives

Questionnaire for Course Data Collection:

  • Design a questionnaire to collect data on the courses users have taken, their academic background, and areas of interest
  • Data collected from the questionnaire helps personalize the career exploration experience by providing users with relevant resources, job recommendations, and skill development opportunities based on their academic background and interests

Result

  • Enhanced User Confidence
    User satisfaction surveys revealed an 85% increase in confidence levels in career decision-making process.
  • Streamlined decision-making process for users
    A/B testing demonstrated a 20% increase in click-through rates for call-to-action buttons


Lessons Learned

One of the key lessons learned from this project is the value of incorporating 3D design to create interactive simulations. While the project achieved its goals of simplifying complex career information and appealing to Gen Z's interests, the addition of 3D design could have enhanced the user experience even further.

Key Takeaway

    Initially, the platform's design lacked adequate consideration for users with disabilities, leading to usability issues and exclusion of certain user groups

    Through user feedback and accessibility audits, it became evident that accessibility features were crucial for ensuring inclusivity and usability for all users.

    As a result, the design team prioritized integrating features such as screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, and color contrast adjustments.
